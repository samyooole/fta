 
 
ANNEX   I 
 
Schedule of Chile 
 
Headnote  
 
Elimination of Customs Duties on Goods Originating 
in Brunei Darussalam, New Zealand or Singapore  
 
 
 
Introductory notes 
 
I.  The tariff schedule in this Annex contains the following five columns: 
 
(a)  “Code”: the code used in the nomenclature of the Harmonized System 
2002. 
 
(b)  “Description”: description of the product falling under the heading. 
 
(c)  “Base  Rate”:  the  basic  customs  duty  from  which  the  tariff  elimination 
programme starts. 
 
(d)  “Category”: the category or categories under which the product concerned 
falls for the purposes of tariff elimination.  
 
(e)  “Observation”: Additional information. 
 
II.  Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, Chile shall progressively 
eliminate  its  customs  duties  on  originating  goods  of  the  other  Parties  in 
accordance with its Tariff Schedule set out in this Annex.  The base rate and the 
interim rate of customs duty at each stage of reduction for an item are indicated 
for the item in the Schedule.  Reductions shall occur upon entry into force of the 
Agreement and thereafter on 1 January of each year, as provided for in the 
Schedule. 
 
III.  Sugar treatment 
 
Preferential tariff treatment established in Chile’s Tariff Schedule set out in this 
Annex shall be accorded to a quantity of goods equal to the amount of the trade 
surplus  by  volume  from  all  sources  of  Brunei  Darussalam,  New  Zealand  or 
Singapore  respectively,  in  the  following  goods:  HS1701.11,  HS1701.12, 
HS1701.91,  and  HS1701.99.    The  respective  trade  surpluses  of  Brunei 
Darussalam, New Zealand or Singapore will be calculated using the most recent 
annual data available.
