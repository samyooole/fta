 
 
UNDERSTANDING ON ARTICLE 4 OF THE AGREEMENT ON TRADE IN GOODS 
UNDER THE FRAMEWORK AGREEMENT ON COMPREHENSIVE ECONOMIC 
COOPERATION  BETWEEN  THE  ASSOCIATION  OF  SOUTHEAST  ASIAN 
NATIONS AND THE REPUBLIC OF INDIA 
 
The Governments of Brunei Darussalam, the Kingdom of Cambodia (Cambodia), the 
Republic of Indonesia (Indonesia), the Lao People’s Democratic Republic (Lao PDR), 
Malaysia, the Union of Myanmar (Myanmar), the Republic of the Philippines (the 
Philippines),  the  Republic  of  Singapore  (Singapore),  the  Kingdom  of  Thailand 
(Thailand) and the Socialist Republic of Viet Nam (Viet Nam), Member States of the 
Association of Southeast Asian Nations (collectively, “ASEAN” or “ASEAN Member 
States”, or individually, “ASEAN Member State”) and the Government of the Republic 
of India (India) refer to the Agreement on Trade in Goods under the Framework 
Agreement on Comprehensive Economic Cooperation between the Association of 
Southeast Asian Nations and the Republic of India (as amended), and set out below 
their common understanding: 
 
The Parties affirm their understanding that the commitments under Article 4 of the 
Agreement on Trade in Goods do not prevent a Party from taking measures in 
conformity with Article 12 of the Agreement on Trade in Goods. 
 
The  Parties  further  note  their  understanding  that  such  measures  may  include 
measures based on domestic laws and regulations taken in accordance with the 
Basel Convention on the Control of Transboundary Movements of Hazardous Wastes 
and  their  Disposal,  adopted  in  Basel,  Switzerland  on  22  March  1989  or  other 
agreements as provided for in Article 16 of the Agreement on Trade in Goods.
